Syllabus

Phase 1 — Days 1–4 · The Core Install

Day 1 — Phonics + Present Tense

  • French alphabet and pronunciation rules (nasal vowels, silent letters, liaisons)
  • Présent indicatif: -er, -ir, -re verbs
  • Top 20 irregular verbs: être, avoir, aller, faire, pouvoir, vouloir, savoir, venir, voir, prendre + 10 more
  • Anki: Install French Core 2000 deck — do cards 1–100
  • Resource: Français Authentique (YouTube) — Episodes 1–5

Day 2 — Past Tenses

  • Passé composé: avoir + être auxiliaries, past participle agreement
  • Imparfait: formation and usage contrast with passé composé
  • Plus-que-parfait (past perfect)
  • Passé récent: venir de + infinitif
  • Anki: cards 101–200
  • Write 10 sentences about your past in French

Day 3 — Future + Conditional

  • Futur simple: regular + irregular stems
  • Futur proche: aller + infinitif
  • Conditionnel présent: formation and polite use
  • Conditionnel passé: regrets and hypotheticals
  • Si clauses: Type 1, 2, 3
  • Anki: cards 201–300

Day 4 — Subjunctive + Core Vocab Consolidation

  • Subjonctif présent: formation and key triggers (vouloir que, il faut que, bien que…)
  • Subjonctif passé
  • Negation: ne…pas, ne…plus, ne…jamais, ne…que, ne…rien, ne…personne
  • Question forms: inversion, est-ce que, in-situ
  • Anki: cards 301–400 + review 1–300
  • Core topic vocabulary clusters: Family, Food, Travel, Work, Health
Phase 2 — Days 5–14 · Skill Immersion (8h/day, 4 skills × 2h)

Reading (1hr blocks)

  • Days 5–7: TV5Monde articles (B1) + Le Monde Facile
  • Days 8–10: Short stories — "Le Petit Prince" chapters 1–8
  • Days 11–14: Le Monde / France 24 — authentic news articles
  • Technique: read once for gist, annotate unknowns, re-read for detail
  • Build personal vocab list from readings (50 words/day)

Writing (1hr blocks)

  • Days 5–7: Paragraph writing — descriptions (150 words)
  • Days 8–10: Opinion essays — introduce thesis + 2 arguments (200 words)
  • Days 11–14: Formal letters and emails (standard TCF/TEF formats)
  • Tool: LanguageTool.org for free grammar checking
  • Post on iTalki Notebook for native corrections

Listening (1hr blocks)

  • Days 5–7: Inner French Podcast — episodes 1–7 (B1 level, slow clear French)
  • Days 8–10: News in Slow French — intermediate series
  • Days 11–14: RFI Savoirs, France Inter short clips, YouTube: Easy French
  • Technique: listen once without subtitles → transcribe key points → verify
  • Shadow 5 min of audio per session (repeat aloud at native speed)

Speaking (1hr blocks)

  • Days 5–7: Pimsleur French 1 (free via library app Libby)
  • Days 8–10: iTalki community tutors (affordable) — 2×30min sessions
  • Days 11–14: Record yourself on common TCF topics. Listen back critically.
  • Core topics: se présenter, opinion sur l'environnement, technologie, éducation
  • Shadowing: 10min/day — repeat podcast audio verbatim
Phase 3 — Days 15–24 · Skill + Exam (4h+4h)

First 4 Hours — Skill Practice

  • 1h Reading: Authentic B2-C1 texts — Le Monde editorials, literary excerpts
  • 1h Writing: TCF-format essays (synthesis writing, article de blog, lettre formelle)
  • 1h Listening: France Culture podcasts, TED-style conferences in French
  • 1h Speaking: Record 3-min monologue on a new topic daily. Compare to model.

Second 4 Hours — Exam Practice

  • TCF Canada / TEF Canada format familiarisation
  • Timed reading comprehension sets (20 questions / 60 min)
  • Timed listening sets — use official YouTube practice audio
  • Writing: 1 timed essay under exam conditions
  • Review all errors immediately; categorize by grammar / vocab / strategy
  • Free resource: CLE International TCF practice (archive.org)
Phase 4 — Days 25–40 · Exam First (4h exam + 4h skill)

First 4 Hours — Full Mock Exams

  • Full TCF/TEF simulation under strict timed conditions
  • Rotate: Mon/Wed/Fri full exam — Tue/Thu/Sat/Sun section drills
  • Track score per section: target CLB 7 in each
  • Free mock tests: see Mock Exams page
  • Analyse performance trends — double down on weakest skill

Second 4 Hours — Consolidation

  • 1h: Target weakness from morning exam
  • 1h: Vocabulary — Anki + high-frequency C1 word list
  • 1h: Listening or reading (alternate days)
  • 1h: Speaking — iTalki session or self-recording + review
  • Final week: only exam simulations + targeted micro-practice

Technique: Shadowing Protocol

  1. Pick a 2-minute audio clip at your level
  2. Listen once without looking at transcript
  3. Listen again, read transcript simultaneously
  4. Pause every sentence. Repeat aloud verbatim.
  5. Play full clip again — shadow without pausing
  6. Do this 10min/day minimum from Day 5

Reading Technique

  1. Scan — title, headings, first sentence of each paragraph
  2. Read for gist — don't stop for unknown words
  3. Annotate — circle max 10 unknown words per article
  4. Look up — only the most frequent-seeming unknowns
  5. Re-read — now with comprehension
  6. Summarize — write 3 sentences about the article in French
