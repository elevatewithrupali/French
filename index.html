Syllabus
Phase 1 — Days 1–4 · The Core Install

Day 1 — Phonics + Present Tense

  • Alphabet, nasal vowels, silent letters, liaisons
  • Présent indicatif: -er, -ir, -re verbs (conjugation patterns)
  • Top 20 irregular: être, avoir, aller, faire, pouvoir, vouloir, savoir, venir, voir, prendre + 10 more
  • Anki: Install French Core 2000 deck — do cards 1–100
  • Français Authentique YouTube — Episodes 1–5

Day 2 — Past Tenses

  • Passé composé: avoir + être auxiliaries, past participle agreements
  • Imparfait: formation and usage contrast with passé composé
  • Plus-que-parfait (past perfect)
  • Passé récent: venir de + infinitif
  • Anki cards 101–200 · Write 10 sentences about your past

Day 3 — Future + Conditional

  • Futur simple: regular + irregular stems (aller→ir-, avoir→aur-…)
  • Futur proche: aller + infinitif
  • Conditionnel présent: formation and polite use
  • Conditionnel passé: regrets and hypotheticals
  • Si-clauses Types 1, 2, 3 · Anki cards 201–300

Day 4 — Subjunctive + Consolidation

  • Subjonctif présent: formation + key triggers (vouloir que, il faut que, bien que…)
  • Subjonctif passé
  • Full negation: ne…pas/plus/jamais/que/rien/personne
  • Question forms: inversion, est-ce que, in-situ
  • Anki 301–400 + review 1–300 · Topic clusters: Family, Food, Travel, Work, Health
Phase 2 — Days 5–14 · Skill Immersion (4 skills × 2h daily)

Reading (1h blocks × 2)

  • Days 5–7: TV5Monde articles (B1) + Le Monde Campus
  • Days 8–10: Le Petit Prince — chapters 1–8
  • Days 11–14: France 24 / Le Monde — authentic news
  • Scan → gist read → annotate 10 unknowns → re-read → 3-sentence French summary
  • 50 new vocab words extracted daily

Writing (1h blocks × 2)

  • Days 5–7: Paragraph descriptions (150 words)
  • Days 8–10: Opinion essays — thesis + 2 arguments (200 words)
  • Days 11–14: Lettre formelle, courriel professionnel
  • LanguageTool.org for grammar checking (free)
  • Post on iTalki Notebook for free native corrections

Listening (1h blocks × 2)

  • Days 5–7: Inner French Podcast episodes 1–7 (B1)
  • Days 8–10: News in Slow French — intermediate
  • Days 11–14: RFI Français Facile, France Inter clips, Easy French YouTube
  • Listen cold → transcribe key points → verify with transcript
  • Shadow 10min per session (repeat aloud at native speed)

Speaking (1h blocks × 2)

  • Days 5–7: Pimsleur French 1 via Libby (free with library card)
  • Days 8–10: iTalki language exchange — 2×30min per session
  • Days 11–14: Record 3-min monologues on TCF topics. Listen back critically.
  • Core topics: se présenter, environnement, technologie, éducation
  • Shadowing: 10min/day — repeat podcast audio verbatim
Phase 3 — Days 15–24 · Skill + Exam (4h + 4h)

First 4 Hours — Skill Practice

  • 1h Reading: Le Monde editorials, literary excerpts (B2–C1)
  • 1h Writing: TCF essays — synthèse, article de blog, lettre formelle
  • 1h Listening: France Culture, TED conferences en français
  • 1h Speaking: Recorded 3-min monologue. Self-evaluate vs model response.

Second 4 Hours — Exam Practice

  • TCF Canada / TEF Canada format deep-familiarisation
  • Timed reading comprehension (20 questions / 60 min)
  • Timed listening sets — official YouTube TCF audio
  • 1 timed essay under real exam conditions (no aids)
  • Review ALL errors: categorize grammar / vocab / strategy
  • CLE International TCF prep books — free on Internet Archive
Phase 4 — Days 25–40 · Exam First (4h exam + 4h skill)

First 4 Hours — Full Mock Exams

  • Full TCF/TEF simulation — strict timed conditions, no pausing
  • Rotate: Mon/Wed/Fri full exam · Tue/Thu/Sat/Sun section drills
  • Track score per skill: target CLB 7 in each component
  • Trend analysis: chart your weekly score progression
  • Final week: exam-only sessions, no skill practice

Second 4 Hours — Consolidation

  • 1h: Target the skill where you lost most points in AM exam
  • 1h: Vocabulary — Anki + C1 high-frequency word list
  • 1h: Listening or reading (alternate days)
  • 1h: Speaking — iTalki or self-recording + critical review

🔄 Shadowing Protocol
  1. Choose a 2-minute clip at your current level
  2. Listen once — no transcript, no subtitles
  3. Listen again while reading the transcript
  4. Pause every sentence. Repeat aloud verbatim. Match the rhythm.
  5. Play the full clip again and shadow without pausing
  6. Minimum 10 minutes of shadowing daily from Day 5

📖 Reading Protocol
  1. Scan — title, headings, first sentence of each paragraph
  2. Gist read — don't stop for unknown words. Get the shape.
  3. Annotate — circle maximum 10 unknowns per article
  4. Look up — only the most frequent-seeming unknowns
  5. Re-read — now with comprehension and context
  6. Summarize — write 3 sentences about the article in French

  • Courriel formel — professional email with standard codes
  • Article de blog / opinion — thesis + 2-3 arguments + conclusion
  • Compte-rendu — factual summary of a document or situation
  • Synthèse (C1) — synthesize 2-3 source documents
